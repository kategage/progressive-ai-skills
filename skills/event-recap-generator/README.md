# Event Recap Generator

**Category:** Content & Comms
**Effort to set up:** 5 minutes
**Tested with:** Claude Code, Cowork

## What it does

Takes notes, a transcript, or a summary from an event, webinar, panel, or workshop and generates a shareable recap. Outputs multiple formats: a full recap (for blog/website), a short version (for newsletter/email), and social posts. Designed for the kinds of events progressive orgs host -- panels, webinars, study halls, open mics, trainings.

## Who it's for

Comms staff, event organizers, and anyone who hosts events and needs to share what happened with a broader audience. Especially useful for organizations that host recurring events (like HGI's AI Open Mics or Study Halls) and need consistent, fast recaps.

## Example

**Input:** Notes or transcript from a 60-minute webinar on AI tools for campaigns

**Output:**

Three formats delivered together:

1. **Full recap** (300-500 words) -- structured with key takeaways, speaker highlights, and notable quotes. Ready for a blog post or website.

2. **Short version** (100-150 words) -- the core takeaways in a tight paragraph. Ready for a newsletter blurb or email.

3. **Social posts** -- 2-3 platform-ready posts (Twitter/X length, LinkedIn length, Bluesky length) highlighting the most compelling moments.

## Customization

You can customize the SKILL.md to match your organization's voice, add specific sections you always include (like "resources mentioned" or "upcoming events"), or adjust the output formats for your distribution channels.

## How to use

Drop the SKILL.md into your skills directory. Then share event notes or a transcript and say something like "write up a recap of this event" or "turn this into a blog post and social posts." The skill will activate automatically.
